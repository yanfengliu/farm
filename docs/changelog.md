# Changelog

## Unreleased

- Ringed the world with a border woodland: panning or zooming to any edge now meets a deep, varied treeline — birches to the north, willows south, elders and willows beyond the creek, apples and hazels east — thinning naturally toward the open meadow heart. Around two hundred deterministic trees keep the framed farm view exactly as it was, stay off the creek, respect the organic art grammar, and can be pinned by Farm Notes like any other tree. The default recenter contract now states its intent precisely: no tree may straddle the framed view's edge as a half-tree, whether it lives inside or outside.
- Renamed the bee skeps to "Straw Beehives" after a second player note captured the correct "Bee Skeps" label and still had to ask what it was — knowing the word "skep" cannot be a prerequisite for a label helping. Inspect now answers the question directly: clicking any wild meadow story (the hay bales, the scarecrow, the beehives) introduces it by name with a one-line description, notes that it is a wild meadow story on unowned land, and explains that buying the land clears it, while keeping the usual purchase guidance. Plain locked land inspects exactly as before.
- Acted on a player Farm Note asking "What is this?" about the golden shape south of the farm: the hay bales. Recut all three southern meadow vignettes from stacked solid rectangles into the organic pixel grammar — two round coiled straw bales with loose wisps, two ridged dome bee skeps with entrance arches, boards, and drifting bees, and a scarecrow with stepped shoulders, a pinched waist, and a tattered hem — each over broken ground shadows. A raster contract now holds every vignette to the same no-solid-block grammar as the stones and plants.
- Farm Notes now name the southern vignettes: a note pinned on those wild cells captures "Hay Bales", "Scarecrow", or "Bee Skeps" instead of anonymous "Wild Land", exactly as the hedgerow is named — so the note that asked this question would now answer itself. Notes pinned on farmhands also use their names: a pin on the first farmhand reads "Fern", not "Farmhand 1". Purchased vignette cells still fall back to real tile labels because the stories yield to owned land. — Fern, Alder, Poppy, and Rowan — and inspecting one introduces them with a framed pixel portrait drawn from the exact same art the scene renders, above their task, target, cargo, and route details. Names are content-only projection, so existing saves gain them with no migration.
- Farm work is now celebrated where it happens: planting kicks up a small soil dust puff, watering scatters falling droplets, and harvests release rising harvest-gold sparkles at the working farmhand. Effects are presentation-only — spawned from stat changes, animated on presentation time, capped, and never written to saves or replay — and a paused farm spawns none while in-flight pixels finish.
- Storybook chrome accents: panel headings carry a stepped harvest-gold underline, HUD chips a subtle pixel frame, and the Coins chip pulses gold when the total changes. All accents are layout-neutral, verified untruncated at 1280x800 and 1024x720.
- Raised every browser test's inline budget from 15s to 30s. The slowest tests measure 10-12 seconds of real solo work, and the suite's growth to 56 files made 15s budgets a scheduling coin flip — a clean-checkout baseline proved the flakes predate this feature work. Hangs still fail; contention no longer does.
- Player edits now apply immediately while the farm is paused. Commands queue into the deterministic engine and only apply during a simulation step, and a paused farm never steps on its own — so placing a well, buying seeds, or pressing Undo while paused looked completely dead, then every queued click landed at once on resume. A paused farm now advances exactly one recorded tick per player command: the edit is visible the moment it is made, autonomous work stays halted, and the tick is recorded normally so replay and history are unaffected. Seed purchases and crop sales remain outside the undo domain, as designed.
- Gave the recursive playtest loop a save/reload restore oracle: after all other evidence is exported, every visual-loop run now reloads the page like a returning player and requires the restored farm to be the same farm the autosave recorded, with identity rules held exactly and progress only ever moving forward. Any loss — including the game refusing its own save — raises a critical `autosave-reload-lost-state` finding, resurrecting the orphaned P1 oracle from the deprecated scripted harness. The loop's storage clear is now guarded to once per browser context, since an unguarded per-navigation clear would wipe the very autosave the check proves; the recursive pass declares the new lane in its discovery scope.
- Added end-to-end browser coverage for canvas tool placement: real clicks at calibrated screen coordinates now provably place a well, place a storage bin (with the HUD capacity chip following), buy adjacent land (with the HUD coin chip following), and bulldoze back to empty — closing the gap where these tools were only ever exercised as direct simulation commands.
- Added browser-boot coverage for legacy saves: a pre-ecology, pre-Village-Harvest save now provably loads through the real page, receives every additive migration (starter ducks and fish, community state, upgrades, pumpkin entries), renders, and is written back as a current save — so a future change that breaks old-save migration fails a test instead of a returning player.
- Shortened the autosave-failure and reset-failure HUD warnings so they read in full at the smaller supported desktop size. `.hud-alert` ellipsizes rather than wraps, and at 1024x720 the autosave message truncated to "Autosave unavailable - progress remains in this..." — cutting exactly the half that tells the player to free browser storage. Both now fit at 1280x800 and 1024x720, and a contract measures the rendered width so a future message cannot silently lose its actionable half. The existing failure assertions only ever read `textContent`, which keeps the whole string regardless of how it renders, which is why this went unnoticed.
- A saved farm this build cannot read is no longer destroyed without a word. Loading now separates "no save yet" from "a save is here but it cannot be read": a genuine first run still starts a new farm quietly, while an unreadable save is left exactly where it is, autosave stops rather than overwriting it, and the HUD says so and names Reset as the way out. Reset drops the save and resumes autosave, which is the player consenting to lose it. Previously a save that failed JSON parsing or state validation was indistinguishable from a first run, so the new farm's autosave replaced it about a second later and the player saw nothing at all.
- Added the first coverage anywhere that a played farm actually survives a real browser reload, alongside contracts for the unreadable-save notice, byte-exact quarantine, and the absence of a false warning on a first run. Reload restore had only ever been exercised headlessly against an in-memory storage double; the browser lane that once checked it was lost when the scripted playtest harness was retired, and the visual loop cannot reach the restore path at all because it clears storage on every navigation.

- Guarded playtest proof frames against browser compositor corruption. Visual-loop capture now measures opaque-black coverage plus the longest unbroken black row and column on every frame and recaptures when a frame reads as corrupt, so an archived screenshot cannot silently claim to show a farm it never rendered. The whole observe-and-shoot sequence is retried rather than the screenshot alone, keeping each accepted frame paired with the DOM sample taken microseconds before it. Exhausted retries keep the least-corrupt frame, mark it degraded, and raise a `screenshot-compositor-degraded` finding instead of discarding a completed run's report, bundle, and ledger row over one bad frame. Clean frames stay unannotated, so the packet gains nothing on the normal path.

- Replaced broad opaque green fills across trees, berry shrubs, ferns, cultivated carrots, wheat, tomatoes, pumpkins, clover, cottage beds and window boxes, climbing vines, creek lilies, cattails, sedges, iris blooms, and every authored hedgerow with reusable one-pixel leaf sprays, bent stems, separated grain heads and petals, visible woody structure, negative space, crooked crowns, uneven baselines, blossoms, and ground growth. Creek-bank communities now vary their species clusters and silhouettes without a strict alternating rhythm. Recut meadow rocks, cottage stepping stones, and the southern path from grey slabs into asymmetric shouldered silhouettes with mineral facets, cracks, lichen, and broken ground shadows, and broke the cottage's rigid grey smoke tiles into translucent drifting pixel wisps; ground-level pavers now sit behind the raised laundry. Exact scenery bounds move the north hedge above the playable grid, keep the western elder on the dry creek bank, place the garden sign below rather than over the beds, and clear the Tier 4 harvest stall from its willow, foxglove, and mushroom neighbors. Farm Notes still identify the east graphic as Wild Hedgerow, and the default camera keeps the complete thicket visible at both supported desktop sizes.

- Added Point and Box modes to Farm Notes. Clicking either shape now arms it immediately; Box is a mouse-drag-only freeform selection with continuous-pixel size and position, a 12 × 12 px accidental-drag threshold, live dimensions, reverse/off-canvas drag support, persistent camera-aware outlines, undistorted evidence previews, strict coordinate validation, legacy point-note compatibility, debug/LLM context, and responsive desktop layouts. Box drafts and saved records identify the selected area's dimensions with its center cell only as secondary context. The deterministic visual player discovers both modes, performs a real bounded drag, requires observed draft evidence before advancing, releases the pointer after failed drags, and keeps annotation gestures separate from gameplay-paint coverage.
- Hardened Farm Notes keyboard ownership so every Phaser movement key and gameplay shortcut remains native in draft and edit textareas, including both keydown and keyup for WASD, arrows, Home, Space, and Shift. Those keys still control the farm when gameplay owns focus; only `Escape` cancel and `Ctrl`/`Cmd` + `Enter` save are intentionally consumed by the editor.
- Added Farm Notes: press `N`, click any world detail or press `Enter` to capture the canvas center, write a comment, and keep a numbered camera-aware pin with edit, view, copy, export, confirmed delete, reset-context, local persistence, exact state/camera/coordinate capture, evidence crop, and public debug-bundle support.
