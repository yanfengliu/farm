## Player report: flickering panels and hijacked inspection

- A four-part player report, one root cause and one wrong model. The flicker (upgrade buttons on hover, the Goals page, "dynamic content that comes and goes too quickly"): every live-data change forced a full `innerHTML` rewrite of the panel, replacing the node under the cursor and restarting its hover transition — the Goals signature includes coins and per-crop lifetime counts, so this fired on nearly every farm heartbeat. The hijack: Inspect selection was cell-based and the panel looked up "who stands on this cell" live, so a farmhand crossing the selected tile replaced the inspection until he left.
- Flicker fix: `src/ui/domMorph.ts`, a ~70-line DOM patcher — recursive child sync, attribute diff, text-node updates, canvas protection, and live input-value handling (blurred controls follow state, the focused control keeps the player's editing). `renderPanel` morphs instead of rewriting. Contracts red-first at two levels: five morph unit contracts (identity preservation, attribute round-trip, structural add/remove, painted canvas + blurred input survival, focused input protection) and two whole-app browser contracts that mark real Goals/Inventory buttons and require the same element instances after a minute of live farm churn — both failed against `innerHTML`, green after the morph.
- Selection fix: selection is decided at click time and explicit. Clicking a farmhand's tile selects that hand by id — sticky as he moves, marked with warm corner brackets in the interaction lane; clicking anything else selects the cell, and `inspectMarkup` no longer looks up workers at all for cell targets, so a passing hand cannot hijack the panel. A dedicated Farmhands tab (new `farmhand` pixel icon, `src/ui/farmhandsPanel.ts`) lists every hand with portrait, name, live task, cargo, and position; roster rows toggle the same selection. Red-first unit contracts pin cell-selection-ignores-workers, sticky worker selection across movement, graceful missing-id fallback, and roster markup; browser contracts drive the real click paths.
- The morph exposed a latent Farm Notes bug that had been passing by accident: `annotationBoundingBox`'s "ignores Enter until the active drag ends" failed consistently. Instrumentation showed the drag healthy; the difference was the test's Enter press. Pre-morph, the panel rewrite destroyed the focused Box button, dropping focus to body, so Enter fell through to the drag guard. The morph correctly preserves the button — so Enter activated it, `setMode` ran, and `cancelPointerSelection` silently killed the player's held drag. Enter is now inert during an active drag regardless of focus, which is what the test's name always claimed. A real player could have hit this exact loss.
- `farmUiController` crossed its 500-line ceiling during the wiring, so `goalsMarkup` moved to `src/ui/goalsPanel.ts` (497 after). Verification: gates pass 63 of 63 files twice consecutively, typecheck, lint, build clean; roster and ring captured at 1280x800 and 1024x720 with zero panel overflow.

## Border woodland: forest at every edge of the world

- Player direction: the outer screen should be covered in lots of trees. Implemented as a deterministic woodland ring in `farmBotany.ts` between the framed view and the legal camera world (14 tiles of margin on every side): a 52px lattice with hash jitter, presence probability thinning from 88% near the world edge to 30% near the frame, side-weighted species pools, and per-candidate fit rejection against the environment bounds, the frame plus 26px breathing, and the meandering creek column. Roughly 230 trees join `layout.trees`, so they draw through the existing ground/overstory lanes, sort with authored trees, and are automatically pickable by Farm Notes ("Birch Tree") through the unchanged resolver loop.
- Red-first contract `farmBorderWoodland.test.ts`: at least 120 border trees with minimum counts on all four sides, every visual bound inside the camera world and fully clear of the framed view, the creek open along its whole run, at least four species, no duplicate positions, jittered rather than grid-aligned placement, determinism, and untouched groves/shelters.
- Two pre-existing contracts met the woodland honestly. The botany grammar test overflowed the call stack on `Math.min(...spread)` once the layout recorded ~230 trees of leaf sprays — the helper now loops; the grammar itself validates the entire woodland in 620ms. And the recenter contract required every tree wholly inside the framed view, encoding the old sixteen-tree world; its intent — no half-tree straddling the default crop edge — is now stated directly: wholly inside, or wholly outside with no intersection. That is a deliberate design-driven contract change, not a relaxation: straddling still fails from either side.
- The full-suite run also exposed a latent race in the work-effects purity contract from yesterday's enrichment: autosave trails state by its one-second throttle, so snapshotting save bytes immediately after pausing could catch the pre-pause write and then legitimately observe the throttled rewrite of the frozen state inside the comparison window. The test now waits for the save's tick to equal the frozen state's tick before snapshotting. The woodland's one-time draw cost shifted boot timing enough to surface it; the fix makes the contract deterministic rather than timing-lucky. The remaining inline 20s browser budgets (10 across five files) joined the 30s/40s sweep for the same reason as before.
- Verification: zoomed-out capture shows the full ringed world with the farm heart untouched; the default framed view is pixel-familiar. Gates pass 59 of 59 files twice consecutively with typecheck, lint, and build clean.

## The 15-second browser budget convention died at 56 files

- The enrichment's gate run surfaced rotating 15s timeouts across browser suites — different files each run, all passing in isolation. Lowering the fork cap to 4 did not help, which killed the pure-load theory: the suite's slowest tests measure 10-12 seconds of genuine solo work (cold per-file Vite transform plus canvas pixel scans), and with 22 Chromium-launching files sharing 6 forks, long suites pin forks and stack the tail past any 15s budget.

- Fix: one mechanical sweep of every inline `}, 15000)` in `tests/browser/` to `}, 30000)` (22 raised in the sweep, plus the five chronic files raised while diagnosing).
